🦉 OTTO TIP

Screens don't use ink—they use light. Red, Green and Blue light add together to create brighter colours. When all three overlap at full intensity, the result is white.

💡 DID YOU KNOW?

  • RGB is used by cameras, scanners and monitors.
  • Printing presses reproduce colour using CMYK, not RGB.
  • RGB can reproduce a wider range of colours than CMYK.
  • Colours that look vibrant on a monitor may not be printable.
